Spot Price vs Real Dealer Payouts

What dealers actually pay for your silver versus the live spot price

There is a crucial distinction between the silver spot price you track on a market chart and the actual cash a dealer will hand you for a piece of scrap silver, a sterling silver necklace, or a hallmarked bullion bar. The spot price is a wholesale benchmark — the rate at which large financial institutions trade standardized contracts for .999 pure silver for immediate delivery on the COMEX and London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) exchanges. It is not a retail price, and it is almost never what any individual seller receives for physical silver.

When you bring silver to a coin shop, an online refiner, or a pawn shop, that dealer must account for several costs before making you an offer: the expense of assessing and testing the hallmark, the labor involved in weighing each gram or pennyweight precisely, refining fees to convert raw scrap silver into a tradeable form, shipping and insurance on the recovered metal, and their own profit margin.

What Moves the Silver Price Daily

The silver spot price responds to six major market forces. Understanding these drivers helps predict price momentum and time your buying or selling decisions.

📊

Industrial Demand

~55% of silver demand comes from industry — solar panels, electronics, electric vehicles.

💵

US Dollar Strength

Stronger USD often puts pressure on silver — quoted globally in USD.

📈

Inflation Expectations

Silver is used as an inflation hedge — rising inflation expectations boost demand.

💰

Investment Flows

ETFs and investors drive major price moves — large fund inflows/outflows shift spot price.

🌍

Geopolitical Events

Global events create safe-haven demand — political tension boosts precious metal prices.

📊

Interest Rates

Rising rates can reduce silver investment — opportunity cost of holding bullion increases.

Silver Price Today — FAQ

What is the silver price today? ▼
Today's silver spot price is $68.84 per troy ounce for .999 fine silver, sourced from COMEX. For sterling silver (.925) it's $63.68 per troy ounce; coin silver (.900) is $61.96; European (.800) is $55.07.
Is the silver spot price the same as dealer payout? ▼
No. The spot price is the wholesale benchmark for .999 pure silver traded in bulk on COMEX and LBMA. Dealers pay 75–98% of melt value depending on purity, quantity, and buyer type. See the dealer payout table above for typical ranges.
How often does the silver price update? ▼
The COMEX spot price updates every minute during market hours (6pm Sunday EST through 5pm Friday EST). This page updates every 60 minutes. Check a live trading platform for real-time intra-second updates.
What affects the daily silver price? ▼
COMEX futures trading volume, USD exchange rate movements, industrial demand signals (solar panels, electronics, EVs), ETF inflows/outflows, and geopolitical events all move the spot price. The per-gram price moves proportionally with spot.
Where can I sell my silver? ▼
Professional refiners (500g+ lots, 90–95% payout), online bullion buyers (APMEX, JM Bullion, SilverRecyclers.com), local coin dealers (same-day cash, 82–90% payout), and coin shows. Avoid pawn shops (50–70% payout) unless desperate.
