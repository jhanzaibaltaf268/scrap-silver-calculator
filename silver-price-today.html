📰 Market Context

Why Does Silver Price Change Every Day?

The major factors driving silver price movements and how to read market signals.

🏭

Industrial Demand

Silver is essential for solar panels, electronics, EVs, and water purification. ~55% of annual demand is industrial. Strong manufacturing in Asia and North America lifts prices; recessions drag them lower.

💵

US Dollar Strength

Silver prices move inversely to the USD. A strong dollar makes silver expensive for foreign buyers, reducing demand. Dollar weakness boosts buying from Europe, Asia, and emerging markets.

📊

Inflation Expectations

Silver is an inflation hedge. When CPI data surprises to the upside or central banks signal loose policy, investment demand for silver rises sharply as investors seek tangible store of value.

📈

Investment Flows & ETFs

Silver ETF holdings, futures positioning, and retail accumulation heavily influence price. "Risk-on" sentiment sends money into silver fast. Social media and retail coordination amplify moves.

🌍

Mine Supply Disruptions

Top silver producers are Mexico, Peru, Russia, and China. Labor strikes, environmental shutdowns, or new mine closures tighten supply overnight. A single major closure can move prices 5–10%.

🏦

Interest Rates & Central Banks

Silver pays no yield, so rising rates reduce appeal vs. bonds. Rate hike cycles historically pressure silver. Fed pivots to cuts or pauses trigger sharp rallies in precious metals.

⚡

Gold/Silver Ratio

This ratio (gold ÷ silver price) typically ranges 60–90. A ratio above 80 signals silver is undervalued relative to gold. Today's ratio: —. Value investors use this to time accumulation.

⚔️

Geopolitical Events

Wars, trade tensions, and sanctions trigger "risk-off" flows into precious metals. Ukraine 2022 spiked silver sharply. Peace signals or de-escalation often reverse the move within days.

📆

Economic Data Releases

US jobs reports, CPI, and GDP figures move silver as they signal future rate direction. Strong employment often pushes silver down; weak data lifts it on rate-cut speculation.

Understanding Spot vs. What You Receive

Spot price is the rate for immediate delivery of pure 999 silver with no premiums. When buying, you pay spot plus a dealer premium (5–20% for bars, more for coins). When selling scrap, you receive spot minus the dealer's discount (typically 5–40%). Shopping 3+ dealers before selling can earn you 20–30% more per ounce.
