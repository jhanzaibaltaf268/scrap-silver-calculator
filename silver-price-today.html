🏦 Dealer Payouts

Spot Price vs Real Dealer Payouts

What dealers actually pay for your silver versus the live spot price

There is a crucial distinction between the silver spot price you track on a market chart and the actual cash a dealer will hand you for a piece of scrap silver, a sterling silver necklace, or a hallmarked bullion bar. The spot price is a wholesale benchmark — the rate at which large financial institutions trade standardized contracts for .999 pure silver for immediate delivery on the COMEX and LBMA exchanges. It is not a retail price, and it is almost never what any individual seller receives.

When you bring silver to a coin shop, an online refiner, or a pawn shop, that dealer must account for several costs: assessing and testing the hallmark, weighing precisely, refining fees to convert raw scrap into tradeable metal, shipping, insurance, and their own margin. The result is a payout between 75% and 98% of melt value depending on purity and buyer type. Online refiners pay highest; pawn shops pay least.

📈 Market Forces

What Moves the Silver Price Daily

Six major forces drive the silver spot price. Understanding them helps you time buying and selling decisions.

🏭
Industrial Demand
~55% of silver demand comes from industry — solar panels, electronics, electric vehicles, and medical equipment. The dominant long-term price driver.
💵
US Dollar Strength
Silver is priced globally in USD. A weaker dollar makes silver cheaper for foreign buyers, lifting demand and price — and vice versa.
📈
Inflation Expectations
Silver is used as a hedge against inflation and currency devaluation. Rising inflation expectations drive safe-haven demand and push prices higher.
💰
Investment Flows
ETFs, institutional investors, and bullion buyers drive major price moves. Large fund inflows/outflows shift the spot price significantly and quickly.
🌍
Geopolitical Events
Global instability creates safe-haven demand — political tension, wars, and financial crises historically boost precious metal prices.
⛏️
Mining Supply
Over 70% of silver is a byproduct of copper, lead, and zinc mining — supply can't respond quickly to price spikes, amplifying upward moves.

❓ FAQ

Silver Price Today — FAQ

How much is scrap silver worth today? +
Scrap silver is worth its silver content multiplied by today's live spot price of $33.24 per troy ounce. The exact value depends on purity (.925 sterling = 92.5% of spot; .999 fine = 99.9%; .900 coin = 90%; .800 European = 80%) and weight. Use the scrap silver calculator for an instant result.
Is the silver spot price the same as dealer payout? +
No. The spot price is the wholesale benchmark for one troy ounce of .999 pure silver traded on COMEX and LBMA. Dealers pay 75–98% of melt value depending on purity, quantity, and buyer type. See the dealer payout table above for today's realistic ranges.
How do I calculate 925 sterling silver price today? +
Multiply your weight in troy ounces by 0.925 (purity) by today's spot price. Example: 10 grams of sterling silver = (10 ÷ 31.1035) × 0.925 × spot price. The quick formula: weight (g) ÷ 31.1035 × 0.925 × spot. For a dedicated tool, see the sterling silver calculator.
What affects the silver price today? +
COMEX futures trading volume, USD exchange rate movements, industrial demand signals (solar panels, electronics, EVs), ETF inflows/outflows, and geopolitical events all move the spot price. The per-gram price moves proportionally with spot.
How often does the silver price update on this page? +
Prices refresh every 60 minutes via COMEX and LBMA data sourced through GoldAPI.io with multiple fallback providers. During market closures (weekends and US holidays), prices remain at the last traded value and resume updating when markets reopen Monday at 6pm EST.
