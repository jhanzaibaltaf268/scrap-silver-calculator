What Is a Scrap Silver Calculator?

A scrap silver calculator determines the intrinsic melt value of a silver item — not its retail price or sentimental value. It answers: if you melted this item down to pure silver today, what would that metal be worth? Our engine utilizes real-time market data to provide the same valuation used by professional refineries.

Melt Value = (Weight × Purity) ÷ 31.1035 × Spot Price

Every calculation is performed with 4-point decimal precision — the same methodology used by major precious metals refineries and commodity trading desks worldwide.

Hallmark Identification Guide

Look for "925" (Sterling), "999" (Fine Silver), or "900" (Coin Silver). These tell you the percentage of silver in the alloy. European pieces show "800" or "835" stamps. Items marked "EPNS" or "Silver Plate" contain only a microscopic layer of silver — these have negligible scrap value.

For a detailed breakdown of global silver markings, visit our Hallmark Identification Guide.

Understanding Market Intelligence

The gold-to-silver ratio shown in our dashboard is a key market indicator. When it exceeds 80, silver is historically undervalued relative to gold. When it drops below 60, silver may be relatively expensive. Dealers and pawn shops often offer "flat rates" that may be significantly below the actual melt value. By using this calculator, you establish a baseline for negotiation. Most reputable buyers pay between 70% and 95% of the values shown here.
