📚 Silver Knowledge

What Is Scrap Silver — and How Is It Valued?

What Is Scrap Silver — and Why Does It Have Value?

Scrap silver is any solid silver object valued for its precious metal content rather than its craftsmanship or collectibility — broken jewelry, mismatched flatware, worn coins, industrial components, even dental silver. If it contains measurable silver, it has real calculable value tied directly to the global spot price.

Silver's value is intrinsic: a bent ring holds the same silver value as a bullion bar of equal weight and purity. Refiners purchase scrap, melt and purify it, then return it to market as investment-grade bullion or industrial feedstock.

What qualifies? Broken jewelry (rings, necklaces, bracelets), sterling flatware (spoons, trays, tea sets), pre-1965 US coins, bullion bars and rounds, antique frames. The critical distinction is solid silver vs. silver-plated. Items marked EPNS, Silver Plate, or Sheffield Plate have only a microscopic coating — negligible scrap value.

Understanding Silver Purity: What 999, 925, 900 and 800 Mean

Purity is measured in parts per thousand. A 999-stamped item is 99.9% pure silver. A 925-stamped piece contains 925 parts silver per 1,000 — the remaining 75 parts are copper for durability. Purity directly multiplies your melt value.

  • 999 Fine Silver — 99.9% pure. Bullion bars, modern investment coins. Highest melt value. Stamped .999 or 999.
  • 925 Sterling Silver — 92.5% pure. Most jewelry, flatware, decorative items worldwide. Stamped 925 or STERLING.
  • 900 Coin Silver — 90% pure. Pre-1965 US dimes, quarters, halves, and dollars. Identified by coin date, not stamp.
  • 800 European Silver — 80% pure. German, Dutch, Italian, and Scandinavian silverware. Stamped 800 with regional marks.

How to Test If Silver Is Real

Not sure if your item is genuine solid silver? Use these three tests before calculating value.

🔍 Check for Hallmarks Look for 925, 999, STERLING, or 800. No stamp usually means plated or base metal.
🧲 Magnet Test Silver is not magnetic. A strong magnet sticking to your item means it is not solid silver.
🧪 Acid Test Kit $10–20 at jeweler supply stores. Gives a definitive purity reading within seconds.
