How Much Is a Silver Cup Worth?

Silver cups, including baby cups, goblets, and trophies, are among the heaviest hollowware items found in estate collections. Because of their significant weight, even a single sterling silver cup can have a high melt value, often ranging from **$50 to $250** or more depending on the current silver spot price. Most vintage silver cups are made from 925 sterling silver.

🏆 Trophy & Baby Cup Tip

Baby cups often have "weighted" bases filled with wax or lead to prevent tipping. When calculating melt value, you must subtract the weight of the non-silver filling. Trophies may also be silver-plated; look for "Sterling" or "925" specifically.

Typical Silver Cup & Goblet Weight Reference

Weights can vary based on thickness and decorative elements. Here are common averages:

  • Small Cordial/Shot Cup: 30 – 60 grams
  • Standard Child's Baby Cup: 80 – 150 grams
  • Wine Goblet/Chalice: 150 – 250 grams
  • Water Picher/Large Cup: 300 – 600+ grams

How to Identify Your Silver Cup

Check the bottom of the cup or the outer rim for hallmarks. Genuine silver will be marked with "925", "Sterling", or "800" (common in Europe). If the cup is marked "Quadruple Plate", "EPC", or "Silver on Copper", it is silver-plated and holds very little raw metal value.

Melt Value vs. Antique Value

A hand-chased silver cup from a famous maker like Gorham, Tiffany, or Kirk Steiff may be worth 2-3 times its silver weight to a collector. If the cup has a unique history or exceptional craftsmanship, consider getting an appraisal before selling it to a scrap dealer.
