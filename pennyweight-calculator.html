What Is a Pennyweight?

A pennyweight (DWT) is a unit of weight used primarily by jewelers and precious metals dealers. It equals 1/20 of a troy ounce or exactly 1.55517 grams. The abbreviation "DWT" comes from the Latin "denarius weight" — the weight of a silver penny in medieval England.

Why Do Jewelers Use Pennyweight?

Many jewelry scales and tradesman scales in the US display weight in pennyweight rather than grams. When a jeweler quotes you a price per DWT for your scrap silver, you need to know how to convert to verify the offer. Pennyweight Conversion Formulas

  • 1 DWT = 1.55517 grams
  • 1 DWT = 0.05 troy ounces (1/20 ozt)
  • 20 DWT = 1 troy ounce
  • 1 gram = 0.6430 DWT
  • 1 troy ounce = 20 DWT = 31.1035 grams
