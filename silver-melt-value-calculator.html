Understanding Silver Melt Value

Silver melt value represents the intrinsic worth of the pure silver metal contained in an item. It's calculated by multiplying the item's weight by its purity percentage and the current spot price of silver.

Melt Value vs. Market Value

Melt value is a baseline — the minimum your silver is worth as raw metal. Some items are worth more than melt:

  • Collectible coins often carry numismatic premiums above melt
  • Artisan jewelry can be worth more due to craftsmanship
  • Antique silverware may have collector value
  • Bullion bars and rounds typically sell close to melt plus a small premium

When Is Melt Value Important?

Melt value matters most when selling damaged, broken, or generic silver items to a dealer or refiner. It sets the floor price — no honest dealer should offer less than 80% of melt for genuine silver.
