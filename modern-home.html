What Is a Scrap Silver Calculator?

At its core, a scrap silver calculator determines the intrinsic melt value of a silver item — not its retail price or sentimental value. it answers one specific question: if you melted this item down to pure silver today, what would that metal be worth? It uses three pieces of information: weight, fineness, and the current spot price of silver.

Understanding Silver Purity Marks

Hallmarks tell the story of your silver's fineness. Common marks include:

  • 999 (Fine Silver) — 99.9% pure, common in bullion.
  • 925 (Sterling Silver) — The global jewelry standard. 92.5% pure silver.
  • 900 (Coin Silver) — Standard for US coins minted before 1965.
  • 800/835 — Common Europea standards for hollowware.

How Melt Value is Calculated

The industry uses the following professional formula for every valuation:

Melt Value = Weight (oz) × Purity (%) × Spot Price ($)

Our dashboard handles this math automatically, converting between units (Grams, Troy Oz, Pennyweight) and applying live data streams from the COMEX and LBMA exchanges.
