What Is Silver Melt Value?

Melt value (also called intrinsic value or metal value) is the worth of a silver item based solely on the amount of pure silver it contains and the current market price of silver. It represents what the silver would be worth if melted down into a pure silver bar.

💡 Simple Definition

Melt value = how much your silver's raw metal is worth, ignoring craftsmanship, design, rarity, or sentimental value.

How Is Melt Value Calculated?

The melt value formula has three components:

📐 Melt Value Formula

Melt Value = Weight (troy oz) × Purity × Spot Price

Step-by-step example:
Item: 50 grams of 925 sterling silver
1. Convert to troy oz: 50 ÷ 31.1035 = 1.608 oz
2. Apply purity: 1.608 × 0.925 = 1.487 oz pure silver
3. Multiply by spot: 1.487 × $32.50 = $48.33

When Is Market Value Higher Than Melt Value?

  • Rare coins — A 1893-S Morgan Dollar is worth thousands, but its melt value is only ~$25
  • Designer jewelry — Tiffany & Co. silver pieces carry brand premiums
  • Antique silverware — Georgian or Arts & Crafts era pieces
  • Limited editions — Proof coins, commemoratives

When Is Melt Value the Best You'll Get?

  • Broken or damaged items
  • Common mass-produced jewelry
  • Scratched, worn, or bent coins
  • Mismatched silverware sets
  • Industrial silver scrap

Why Melt Value Matters When Selling

Knowing the melt value gives you a floor price — the absolute minimum your silver is worth. This is critical because:

  1. Negotiation power — You know the minimum you should accept
  2. Dealer comparison — Compare offers as a percentage of melt value
  3. Avoid getting scammed — Reject any offer significantly below melt
  4. Sell vs. hold decision — Compare melt value against your purchase price using our sell or hold calculator
