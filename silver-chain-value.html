How Much Is a Silver Chain Worth?

A silver chain's value is purely a function of its weight and purity. Because chains come in vastly different thicknesses and lengths, two necklaces of the same length can have very different values. Most jewelry-grade chains are made of 925 sterling silver, an alloy containing 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% copper.

📏 Length vs. Weight

A 20-inch box chain might weigh only 3 grams, while a 20-inch Cuban link chain could weigh over 100 grams. Always weigh your chain on a digital scale in grams to get an accurate melt value.

Typical Silver Chain Weight Reference

If you don't have a scale, you can use these common weight ranges for various chain styles:

  • Thin Rope/Box Chain (16-18"): 2 – 5 grams
  • Medium Herringbone/Snake Chain: 10 – 20 grams
  • Heavy Figaro/Mariner Link: 30 – 50 grams
  • Thick Cuban/Franco Link: 60 – 150+ grams

How to Identify a Genuine Silver Chain

Look closely at the clasp or the small rectangular tag near the closure. You should see a "925" or "Sterling" stamp. Italian-made chains often feature the "925" mark inside an oval border. Beware of "925 CN" marks, which sometimes indicate silver-plated jewelry from certain regions. Real silver is also non-magnetic; if your chain sticks to a magnet, it is likely steel and has no precious metal value.

Calculating the Scrap Melt Value

To find the value yourself, take the weight in grams, multiply by 0.925, and then multiply by the live spot price of silver. Or, use our live calculator above, which automatically pulls the latest market data to give you an instant valuation.
