How Much Is a Silver Necklace Worth?

The value of a silver necklace is primarily calculated by its total weight of precious metal. Most modern silver jewelry is made from 925 sterling silver (92.5% pure silver). For collectors and sellers, knowing the "melt value" is the first step in determining a fair price for scrap jewelry or insurance replacement.

💎 A Note on Gemstones

If your necklace has a pendant with stones (diamonds, turquoise, etc.), you must subtract the weight of the stones to get the accurate silver weight. Most scrap dealers will NOT pay for the weight of semi-precious stones.

Typical Silver Necklace Weight Guide

Weights vary dramatically based on the thickness (gauge) and length of the chain. Here are common industry averages:

  • Thin Box/Cable Chain (18"): 2 – 5 grams
  • Standard Herringbone/Snake Chain: 8 – 15 grams
  • Heavy Figaro/Cuban Link: 25 – 60 grams
  • Ethnic/Statement Collar: 50 – 150+ grams
  • Small Silver Pendant: 2 – 10 grams

How to Identify a Real Silver Necklace

Check the clasp or the small metal tag (hang-tag) near the clasp for the "925" or "Sterling" mark. In Italy, you may see the number "925" inside an oval. If the necklace is marked "GF" (Gold Filled), "RG" (Rolled Gold), or "SP" (Silver Plate), it is not solid silver and has very little scrap value.

Why the Price of Your Necklace Changes

Because the price of silver is traded live on global markets like the COMEX, the value of your jewelry fluctuates daily. Our calculator uses real-time spot prices to ensure you get the most accurate valuation for your silver necklace today.
