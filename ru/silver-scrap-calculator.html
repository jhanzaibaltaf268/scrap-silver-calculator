How to Use the Silver Scrap Calculator

Our Silver Scrap Calculator helps you determine the fair value of your scrap silver before selling to a dealer or refiner. Here's how:

  • Weigh your silver using a kitchen scale or jeweler's scale. Accuracy matters — even a gram can make a difference.
  • Identify the purity by checking for hallmark stamps (925, 999, 800, etc.) on your item.
  • Set dealer discount to estimate what a buyer will actually pay. Most dealers pay 80-95% of melt value.

What Counts as Scrap Silver?

Scrap silver includes any silver item sold primarily for its metal content rather than as a finished product:

  • Broken or damaged jewelry (rings, chains, bracelets)
  • Old or tarnished silverware
  • Silver coins with no numismatic premium
  • Silver dental work or electronics scrap
  • Mismatched or unwanted sterling silver pieces

Tips for Getting the Best Price

  • Know your silver's purity before visiting a dealer
  • Get quotes from multiple buyers
  • Check the spot price on the day you sell
  • Consider selling online to refiners for higher payouts
  • Separate items by purity to get accurate quotes
