How to Use Our Silver Calculators

A complete beginner's guide to every tool on SilverCalc — learn which calculator to use and how to get the most accurate results.

Getting Started: 4 Simple Steps

1

Identify Your Silver

Before calculating value, you need to know what your silver is. Look for a hallmark stamp (925, 800, 999, STERLING). If you're not sure, use our Silver Identifier for testing methods.

2

Weigh Your Item

Use a kitchen scale set to grams for best accuracy. If your scale only shows regular ounces, use our Weight Converter to convert. Need to understand troy vs regular ounces? See What Is a Troy Ounce?

3

Choose the Right Calculator

We have specialized tools for every type of silver. See the complete list below — each calculator is optimized for its category.

4

Enter Details & Get Your Value

Input your weight and purity. The calculator uses live silver spot prices to show the real-time melt value. You can also check prices in 12 different currencies.
