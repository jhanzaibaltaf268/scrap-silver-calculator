How to Calculate Scrap Silver Value

Scrap Silver Value = Weight × Purity × Spot Price
  1. Weigh your silver. Use a digital scale in grams, troy ounces, or pennyweights. Don't confuse troy ounces (31.10 g) with regular ounces (28.35 g).
  2. Identify the purity. Look for a hallmark: 925 (sterling), 999 (fine), 900 (coin silver), 800 (European). No stamp? Use our purity tests.
  3. Apply the live spot price. The calculator auto-fetches today's silver spot price every hour from global commodity markets.
  4. Get melt value & dealer estimate. See the melt value plus the realistic dealer payout (typically 70–95% of melt).

Example: 100g of sterling silver at $30/oz spot = 100g ÷ 31.1035 × 0.925 × $30 = $89.21 melt value.

🔬 Verify First

How to Test If Silver Is Real

  • Check for a hallmark. Genuine silver is stamped 925, 999, STERLING, or 800. No stamp usually means plated or fake.
  • Try a magnet. Silver is not magnetic. If a strong magnet sticks, it isn't solid silver.
  • Use an acid test kit. $10–20 at jeweler supply stores — gives a definitive purity reading.
