How Much Is a Silver Ring Worth?

A typical silver ring weighs between 3 and 12 grams, depending on its size, width, and style. Most modern silver rings are crafted from 925 sterling silver, which contains 92.5% pure silver. To determine the melt value, you multiply its weight in grams by its purity and the current live silver spot price.

💡 Did You Know?

Designer rings by brands like Tiffany & Co. or antique rings from the Victorian era often carry a "premium" value far exceeding their raw melt value. Always check for maker's marks before selling for scrap.

Typical Silver Ring Weight Reference

If you don't have a jewelry scale, you can use these industry averages to estimate your ring's weight:

  • Thin Stackable Band: 1.5 – 3 grams
  • Standard Engagement/Wedding Band: 3 – 5 grams
  • Wide "Chunky" Fashion Ring: 6 – 10 grams
  • Large Men's Signet Ring: 12 – 18 grams
  • Heavy Southwest/Native American Ring: 15 – 25+ grams

How to Identify a Real Silver Ring

Before using our calculator, look for the hallmark. In the United States and Europe, the most common stamp is "925" or the word "STERLING". This is usually located on the inside surface of the band. If you see "EP", "EPNS", or "Silver Plate," the ring has very little silver content and is not worth calculating as scrap.

Why Silver Ring Prices Fluctuate

The value of your ring moves in sync with the global silver market (COMEX). Because silver spot prices change every minute during trading hours, the melt value of your ring today might be different from tomorrow. Our calculator updates using live market data to give you the most accurate estimate possible.
