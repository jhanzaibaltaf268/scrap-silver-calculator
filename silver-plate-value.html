How Much Is a Silver Plate Worth?

In the world of precious metals, "Silver Plate" can be a confusing term. It can refer to a solid silver serving plate (highly valuable) or a silver-plated item (minimal value). On this page, we provide a calculator for the melt value of solid silver plates made of sterling or other fine silver alloys.

🚨 The Most Important Check

99% of "silver plates" found in thrift stores or garages are silver-plated. Before using the calculator, look for the "Sterling" or "925" mark. If you see "EPNS", "Silver on Copper", or "Community", your item has no scrap silver value.

Typical Solid Silver Plate Weight Averages

If you have confirmed your plate is solid sterling silver, you can estimate its value using these common weights:

  • Small Bread/Butter Plate (6"): 120 – 200 grams
  • Standard Salad Plate (8"): 250 – 350 grams
  • Full Dinner Plate (10-12"): 400 – 600 grams
  • Large Service Charger: 600 – 900+ grams

How Purity Affects Plate Value

While 925 Sterling is the most common standard for American and British plates, many European antique plates are made of 800 fine silver (80% pure). Items from the UK might also bear the "Britannia" mark (958 silver). Our calculator allows you to select the precise purity to ensure your melt value estimate is perfectly accurate.

Why Some Plates Shouldn't Be Melted

Hand-hammered silver plates or those with ornate repoussé work from famous 19th-century silversmiths can be worth significantly more than their melt value to collectors. Before selling an heirloom plate for scrap, check for maker's marks like the Tiffany & Co. "M" or Gorham hallmarks. Our tool provides the raw metal floor price for your items.
