How Much Is a Silver Fork Worth?

A sterling silver fork is valued primarily for its precious metal content. Unlike stainless steel, which is worth very little as scrap, a single silver fork can be worth anywhere from **$20 to $60** depending on its weight and the current silver market spot price. Most collectible and estate silver forks are made from 925 sterling silver.

🍴 Weight Note

Forks are typically heavier than teaspoons but lighter than large serving pieces. A full set of 12 dinner forks can contain over 20 troy ounces of pure silver, representing significant value.

Typical Silver Fork Weight Ranges

Silver forks come in various sizes, each with a different average weight:

  • Cocktail/Oyster Fork: 15 – 25 grams
  • Salad/Pastry Fork: 30 – 45 grams
  • Standard Dinner Fork: 45 – 65 grams
  • Large Cold Meat/Serving Fork: 70 – 110+ grams

How to Identify Sterling Silver vs. Silver Plate

Before calculating the value, check the back of the fork handle for hallmarks. Look for "925", "STERLING", or the British "Lion Passant" symbol. If you find marks like "IS" (International Silver), "EPNS", or "Triple Plate," the item is silver-plated. Silver-plated forks have a very thin layer of silver over brass or nickel and have negligible melt value.

Estimating Your Fork's Value

To get an accurate estimate, weigh your forks on a digital scale in grams. Ensure you are weighing only the solid silver pieces. Our calculator uses live COMEX silver prices to provide the most current melt value for your items instantly.
