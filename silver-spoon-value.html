How Much Is a Silver Spoon Worth?

The value of a silver spoon is determined by its weight, its purity (fineness), and the current market spot price of silver. Unlike modern stainless steel flatware, silver spoons are valued by their weight in troy ounces or grams of pure silver content. Most antique and vintage silver spoons are made of 925 sterling silver.

🥄 Identification Tip

Turn the spoon over and look at the back of the handle. If you see "925", "Sterling", or a "Lion Passant" hallmark, it is solid silver. If you see "A1", "EPNS", or "Plate", it is silver-plated and holds very little scrap value.

Typical Silver Spoon Weight Averages

If you don't have a scale, you can use these common weight ranges to estimate the value of your silverware:

  • Salt/Mustard Spoon: 5 – 15 grams
  • Teaspoon: 20 – 35 grams
  • Dessert Spoon: 35 – 50 grams
  • Tablespoon (Soup): 50 – 75 grams
  • Large Serving/Stuffing Spoon: 80 – 120+ grams

Collecting vs. Scraping Silver Spoons

While most damaged or common silver spoons are sold as "scrap" to be melted down, some have significant numismatic or antique value. Early American silversmiths (like Paul Revere) or famous English makers can command prices hundreds of dollars above the melt value. Always research the maker's mark before selling.

How to Calculate the Melt Value

To calculate the value yourself, weigh the spoon in grams, multiply by its purity (0.925 for sterling), divide by 31.1035 to get troy ounces, and multiply by the current spot price. Or, simply use our live calculator above for an instant estimate based on real-time market data.
