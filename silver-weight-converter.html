Silver Weight Units Explained

Troy Ounce vs Regular Ounce

The precious metals industry uses troy ounces (31.1035 g), which are heavier than the standard avoirdupois ounce (28.3495 g). When you see "silver price per ounce," it always means per troy ounce — not a regular ounce.

Pennyweight (dwt)

Jewelers commonly weigh items in pennyweight. There are exactly 20 pennyweight in one troy ounce. One dwt equals approximately 1.5552 grams.

Tola

The tola is used across South Asia (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh) for precious metals trading. One tola equals 11.6638 grams or exactly 3/8 of a troy ounce.

Why Weight Units Matter When Selling Silver

Always confirm which unit a buyer is using. A "per ounce" offer in troy ounces is about 10% more silver than a regular ounce offer — a common and costly source of confusion when selling scrap silver.
