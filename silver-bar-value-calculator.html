How Silver Bar Melt Value Is Calculated

Silver bar melt value is straightforward to calculate:

  1. Convert weight to troy ounces (if in grams, pounds, or kg)
  2. Multiply by purity (e.g. 0.999 for fine silver)
  3. Multiply by today's live spot price

Silver Bar vs Spot Price — Understanding Premiums

Silver bars always trade at a premium above melt (spot) value. The premium covers refining, minting, distribution, and dealer margin. For .999 fine bars, typical premiums are: 1 oz bars: $2–4 over spot; 10 oz bars: $8–15 over spot; 100 oz bars: $50–100 over spot.

When selling back, most dealers pay 95–99% of melt for recognized .999 bars (PAMP, Valcambi, Engelhard, JM Bullion). The melt value shown by this calculator is the floor — your bar is worth at least this.
