How Much Is 1 Ounce of Silver Worth?

The 1 ounce silver bar or coin is the standard unit of investment for silver bullion worldwide. When you see a "silver spot price" on financial news, it refers specifically to the value of one troy ounce (31.1035 grams) of .999 fine silver. Because of its affordable price point and high liquidity, 1oz is the most popular denomination for individual investors.

📏 Troy Ounce vs. Standard Ounce

Precious metals are measured in troy ounces, which are approximately 10% heavier than the standard (avoirdupois) ounce used for groceries. One troy ounce equals 31.1 grams, while a standard ounce is only 28.3 grams. Our calculator always uses the correct troy weight.

Why the 1oz Weight is Critical for Investors

Buying 1oz silver coins (like the American Silver Eagle or Buffalo Round) allows for easy "stacking" and divisibility. If you need to liquidate a small portion of your holding, you can sell individual 1oz units far more easily than a single large 100oz bar. Additionally, 1oz rounds often carry a slightly higher premium over spot than larger bars, but they are also easier to recognize for local coin dealers.

Physical Characteristics of 1oz Silver

A standard 1oz silver round is typically 39mm in diameter and about 3mm thick. Small 1oz bars are approximately 50mm long and 29mm wide. Most 1oz investment silver is refined to .999 fine silver purity, which is the industry standard for investment-grade bullion.
