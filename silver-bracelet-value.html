How Much Is a Silver Bracelet Worth?

Silver bracelets are a popular category of scrap jewelry due to their variety of weights and styles. Whether you have a delicate bangle or a heavy designer cuff, the melt value is determined by the total weight of pure silver it contains. Most fine silver bracelets are made from 925 sterling silver.

⛓️ Style & Weight

A heavy men's Curb or Cuban link bracelet can weigh over 80 grams, making its melt value significantly higher than a standard women's chain. Always weigh your item on a gram scale to get the most accurate result.

Typical Silver Bracelet Weight Benchmarks

Use these average weight ranges to estimate the silver content of your jewelry:

  • Thin Charm Bracelet (Empty): 10 – 15 grams
  • Standard Bangle: 12 – 25 grams
  • Medium ID/Nameplate Bracelet: 20 – 35 grams
  • Heavy Solid Cuff: 40 – 80 grams
  • Chunky Statement Piece: 80 – 150+ grams

How to Identify a Real Silver Bracelet

Look for the "925", "Sterling", or "S925" hallmark, usually found on the clasp or the flat end-tag (tab) of the bracelet. On bangles and cuffs, the mark is often stamped on the inside surface. If the bracelet is magnetic, it is likely steel and has no precious metal value. If it is marked "India" without a purity stamp, it may be "German Silver" or "Nickel Silver," which contain no actual silver.

Why Silver Prices Fluctuate

The price of silver is traded 24 hours a day on international markets. Because spot prices move constantly, the cash value of your silver bracelet will change daily. Our live silver calculator uses real-time market data to give you the most accurate valuation possible for your items.
