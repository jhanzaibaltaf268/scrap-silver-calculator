How Much Is 2 Ounces of Silver Worth?

A 2 ounce silver piece (62.207 grams) is worth exactly twice the current spot price of silver. In the bullion market, 2oz rounds are often referred to as "chunky" or "piedfort" rounds because they are significantly thicker than standard 1oz coins. These larger units are favored by collectors for their high-relief designs and unique physical presence.

💰 Value Calculation

To find the market value of your 2oz silver round, multiply the current live spot price (per troy ounce) by 2. If you are selling to a dealer, expect to receive slightly less than the spot price to account for the dealer's spread.

Why Investors Choose 2oz Silver Rounds

The 2oz silver unit offers a middle ground between the common 1oz round and the larger 5oz bar. Many government and private mints (like the Perth Mint or Intaglio Mint) produce 2oz versions of their most famous series. These pieces are harder to counterfeit than smaller coins due to their weight-to-diameter ratio and are becoming a staple in modern silver "stacking" portfolios.

Specifications of a 2oz Silver Round

While dimensions vary by manufacturer, a standard 2oz silver round is often 39mm to 40mm in diameter—the same width as a 1oz round—but is twice as thick (approx. 6mm). This extra thickness allows for ultra-high-relief (UHR) striking, making the artwork appear to "pop" off the surface of the silver.
