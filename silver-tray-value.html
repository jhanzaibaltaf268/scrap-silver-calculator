How Much Is a Silver Tray Worth?

Silver trays, platters, and salvers are some of the most valuable "scrap" silver items due to their significant surface area and substantial weight. A single large sterling silver tray can weigh several pounds, translating to a melt value of **$500 to $1,500+** at today's silver prices. Most high-quality silver trays found in historical collections are made from 925 sterling silver.

⚠️ Warning: Silver Plate vs. Solid

Many ornate serving trays are silver-plated (silver over copper or brass). These look identical to solid silver but have almost no scrap value. Always look for the word "Sterling" or the "925" mark. If you see "Copper Core" or "EP", it is plated.

Typical Silver Tray Weight Benchmarks

If you don't have a heavy-duty scale, you can use these industry averages to estimate your tray's silver content:

  • Small Bread/Butter Tray: 150 – 300 grams
  • Oval Meat Platter: 600 – 1,000 grams
  • Large Circular Salver: 800 – 1,500 grams
  • Full Tea Service Gallery Tray: 1,500 – 3,000+ grams

Determining the Value of Your Tray

To calculate the melt value, weigh the tray in grams, multiply by 0.925 (for sterling), and then multiply by the live silver market price per gram. Note that some trays have "feet" or handles made of different materials or weighted bases—these should be excluded from the silver weight calculation.

Should You Sell for Scrap or Antique Value?

Trays by legendary makers like Paul Storr, Hester Bateman, or modern luxury brands like Christofle or Buccellati can be worth many times their silver weight. If your tray is in excellent condition and has a clear maker's hallmark, consider an appraisal before melting. Our live silver calculator provides the baseline metal value for your planning.
