How Is the 925 Sterling Silver Price Per Gram Calculated?

The calculation is simple but relies on knowing the current spot price. Sterling silver is an alloy made of 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% other metals (usually copper).

To find the exact melt value per gram:

  1. Take the global spot price of pure silver (priced per troy ounce).
  2. Divide it by 31.1035 to get the price of pure silver per gram.
  3. Multiply that by 0.925 (the purity of sterling silver).

Why Grams Matter for 925 Jewelry

If you are weighing necklaces, rings, or silverware, you are almost definitely using a digital scale that measures in grams. Precious metal dealers and pawn shops also weigh scrap 925 silver in grams before making an offer.
