How Much Is 1/10 Ounce of Silver Worth?

A 1/10 ounce silver round (3.11 grams) is the most common form of "fractional" silver bullion. While the raw metal value is exactly 10% of the current spot price, these tiny coins often trade at a significant premium over spot because of the high relative cost of minting such small items. They are highly sought after by "preppers" and survivalist investors as a portable form of currency for small daily transactions.

🎒 The "Barter" Asset

Fractional silver is often called "survival silver." In a scenario where 100oz bars are too valuable for a loaf of bread, 1/10oz rounds provide a perfect unit of exchange. However, be aware that you will pay 20-50% over the melt value when buying these from a dealer.

Why Investors Buy Fractional 1/10oz Silver

The primary appeal of 1/10oz silver is divisibility. Unlike a 1oz coin or a 10oz bar, which cannot be easily "broken" into smaller pieces, fractional rounds allow you to liquidate small amounts of your wealth as needed. Many private mints produce 1/10oz "Walking Liberty" or "Buffalo" designs that mimic standard American coinage, making them immediately recognizable by look and weight.

Physical Characteristics of 1/10oz Silver

A 1/10oz silver round is very small—typically about 16mm to 19mm in diameter (roughly the size of a US dime or penny). Despite their small size, they are struck from .999 fine silver. Because they are so small and light, they are often sold in tubes of 50 or "sheets" of 20 to prevent loss.
